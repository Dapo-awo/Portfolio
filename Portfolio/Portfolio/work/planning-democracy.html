Redesign of Planning Democracy Website

Website / Brand Identity / UX — Ongoing

Role UX Designer
Type Professional
Status Ongoing
Deliverables Website, Brand Identity, UX Strategy

Overview

Planning Democracy is a Scottish organisation that campaigns for public participation in the planning system. This project involves a full redesign of their digital presence — from the ground up — with a focus on accessibility, clarity, and civic engagement.

The Challenge

The existing website struggled to communicate the organisation's mission clearly to a broad audience. Key issues included:

  • Low accessibility compliance across key pages
  • Poor information hierarchy making it hard for new visitors to understand what Planning Democracy does
  • Outdated visual identity that didn't reflect the organisation's values
  • Limited mobile responsiveness affecting engagement on smaller devices

My Approach

Working closely with the organisation's stakeholders, I conducted discovery research to understand the needs of different user groups — from community activists to local authority planners. This research informed a redesigned information architecture, a refreshed visual identity, and a more accessible interface system.

Key Focus Areas

  • Stakeholder interviews and user research
  • Accessibility audit and WCAG 2.1 compliance planning
  • Brand identity refresh aligned with civic values
  • Responsive design system for web
  • Content strategy and information architecture

This project is currently ongoing. Full case study coming soon.
