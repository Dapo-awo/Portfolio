CityLink: Digital Guide for Student Life in Glasgow

Mobile App / UX/UI — Academic Project

Role UX/UI Designer
Type Academic
Platform Mobile App (iOS)
Deliverables UX Research, UI Design, Prototype

Overview

CityLink is a mobile app designed to help new international and domestic students navigate life in Glasgow. The app consolidates essential city information — transport, housing, social spaces, and student services — into a single, accessible digital guide.

The Problem

New students arriving in Glasgow face significant information overload. City guides are fragmented across multiple websites and apps, making it difficult for students to quickly find what they need during a stressful transition period.

Research

I conducted qualitative interviews with 8 students in their first semester at Glasgow universities. Key pain points identified included:

  • Difficulty understanding the local transport system (SPT, First Bus, Scotrail)
  • Finding affordable social spaces and student discounts
  • Locating university support services quickly
  • Feeling isolated and unsure of where to meet other students

Design Process

Using insight from research, I mapped user journeys, created information architecture, and developed wireframes before producing high-fidelity screens. The design system prioritised clarity and speed — students needed to find information fast, often in unfamiliar surroundings.

Key Design Decisions

  • Tab-based navigation for quick switching between key categories
  • Offline-first content for essential transport and safety information
  • Onboarding flow that personalises the experience based on course and accommodation
  • Accessible colour contrast and large touch targets throughout
